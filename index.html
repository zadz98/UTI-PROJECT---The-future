THE FUTURE: TREATMENTS & PHARMACEUTICALS

So, what do psychedelic drugs do? Well. Research has shown that just a single treatment session with psilocybin has effectively been shown to relieve patients from the hostile grip anxiety has on people suffering mental issues and particularly patients battling terminal cancer types. The use of psychedelic drugs has also been proven to be an effective therapy for substance use disorders.

"When I looked at the history of the 60s and tried to see what kind of mistakes were made, mistakes that we don't want to repeat as we really try 50 years later to reintegrate psychedelics into society. The key message for me is that we have to become the reliable experts both on the risks and the benefits," Rick Doblin. A new study conducted and documented by nature.com, it was shown that magic mushrooms could eventually be used to treat chronic depression. This is possible because of a mechanism described as “resetting” the brain. Basically, psilocybin (the psychedelic substance found in mushrooms) was given to 19 patients over a period of two weeks. The patients were then monitored for an additional five weeks afterwards. From the first week there were clear changes and positive effects.

It showed that Blood flow and brain activity changed positively in areas such as the amygdala, which is the part of the brain associated with feelings of fear and anxiety – this only points to the booming trippy future of treatments. Although brain connectivity decreased (meaning they were not in firm touch with reality) the drug eventually wore off leading to an increase beyond normal levels of non-psychedelic drugs.

According to the Multidisciplinary Association for Psychedelic Studies (MAPS) These discussed results, among many others, were put forward in 2017 at the Psychedelic Science Conference in Oakland, California. This is where ongoing studies and researchers gather every few years to discuss the promising medical future of psychedelics. This includes LSD, psilocybin mushrooms, and MDMA. This field of study has rapidly exploded due to the reforms at the Food and Drug Administration that finally allow these researchers, for the first time in decades, to study the effects of these drugs. This can only make the future brighter for the use and implementation of these substances in medical treatments of patients.

Drug variations & Locations

Drug variations & Locations

Out of the many variations of psychedelic drugs, we picked 10 of them to discuss their treatments and uses, as well as chemical components. The first one on the list is LSD. LSD is one of the most popular drugs that anyone can associate with psychedelics. LSD stands for Lysergic acid diethylamide or acid as some may call it. It had already amassed vast popularity by the 1960s especially amongst the youth and nightlife counterculture. LSD also made its way into the many eyes and eager minds of pop culture, arguably because many popular figures like the Beatles said the used it, big influence. LSD has been said to cause hallucinations. Due to its non-chemically addictive nature, it poses little issues but abusive use may cause some negative effects such as anxiety and paranoia. Research shows that there is an increased likelihood in the near future of it being used to aid mental treatments, although there are no current medical uses.

Psilocybin Mushrooms Psilocybin, or commonly known as magic mushrooms or ‘shrooms’ are a group of fungi that have uses dating back to prehistoric times. Back then used as an entheogen and a form hallucinogen. The psychedelic substance ‘psilocybin’ is found in a wide variety of mushrooms. Psilocybin can cause an increase in empathy and altered thinking. In some species, it can cause open and closed eye visuals, which is normally an array of patterns and colours that last during the ‘trip’. The chemical found in these plants have been used to calm down severe anxiety.

DMT/ Ayahuasca Dimethyltryptamine, better known as DMT, gained its popularity through two researchers in the late 20th century. In the 1990s Rick Strassman (a scientist) studied it extensively and eventually gave it the nickname the Spirit Molecule. Later on Terence Mckenna, an ethnobotanist went on to further his studies and then described the extensive effects of the DMT in detail. DMT’s origin is not certain but it is very possible that it has been used for possibly thousands of years by ancient tribes of the Amazon that use DMT innately in rainforest plants by brewing it in tea. The drug may be amongst the most powerful psychedelic drugs on earth, with severe visual hallucinations. There is little evidence to suggest that it could cause any form of addiction or medical complications. However psychological issues may arise from bad trips.

MDMA Methylenedioxymethamphetamine (who on earth named this????) popularly known as MDMA, ecstasy, or even molly is a well-known entactogen which is a class of drugs that produce a feeling of communion or oneness with others or create a friendly and welcoming vibe. MDMA is most often used as a social drug, often in parties . MDMA results in a rapid increase in emotional connections, this elevation of moods can be used as a form of therapy and sometimes facilitates a mental and physical euphoria

Salvia Salvia, is a psychoactive drug that comes from a plant whos origin comes from the mountains of Oaxaca in Mexico. As opposed to other psychedelic drugs, Salvia is unique in its chemical structure especially once compared to other naturally occurring drugs. It is a broad family of organic chemicals. It distorts sight and sound which gives an illusion of detachment from the world ,their surroundings or even from themselves. It federally legal in the US, however some states like Florida consider it a controlled substance even though it has minimal medical benefits.

Ketamine Ketamine is a also a synthetic drug which is primarily used as an aesthetic surgeries. Due to its strong effect it can easily cause sedation and slight memory loss as a side effect. It is now in veterinary use.

DXM Dextromethorphan, or DXM is the only drug discussed that is solely medical and has already been consumed by many. It Is an active ingredient in a cough syrup and other illness suppressors such as Vicks, Nyquil and Robitussin. At high doses, it has a possibility of causing hallucinogenic effects However, it will not lead to addiction or dependence.
